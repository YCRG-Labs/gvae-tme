Figure 1 (UMAP of GVAE latent space) visualizes the low-dimensional representation learned 
by the GVAE from integrated molecular and relational structure. Cells are projected into a 
two-dimensional manifold and colored by cluster identity, rare-cell score, and patient assignment 
to provide complementary views of latent organization. The cluster panel highlights discrete 
cell-state structure learned from graph-aware encoding, while the rare-score panel shows how
uncertainty-derived rarity is distributed across the manifold rather than confined to a single 
dense region. Together, these views support the interpretation that the GVAE embedding preserves 
biologically meaningful heterogeneity and enables systematic identification of uncommon cellular 
states within the tumor microenvironment.

Figure 2 (spatial distribution of clusters and rare cells) examines how discovered cell states are 
arranged across tissue coordinates and whether high rare-cell scores concentrate in localized niches. 
The left panel maps cluster assignments in physical space to summarize mesoscale tissue architecture, 
and the right panel overlays rare cells on the full cellular background to emphasize potential 
spatially restricted subpopulations. This visualization is intended to test the central hypothesis
that clinically relevant immunosuppressive programs can be both molecularly distinct and spatially 
localized. In this project stage, spatial coordinates used for this figure are placeholder synthetic 
values in the demo analysis script; therefore, Figure 2 should be treated as a layout/template 
description until real spatial metadata are substituted.
